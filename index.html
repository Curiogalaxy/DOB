What is a Due Date?

A due date, also known as the estimated date of delivery (EDD), is the predicted date when a pregnant woman is most likely to give birth.

How it's calculated:

  • Typically calculated as 40 weeks from the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP)
  • This method assumes a 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14
  • Only about 5% of babies are born exactly on their due date
  • Most births occur within two weeks before or after the due date

Your healthcare provider may adjust your due date based on early ultrasound measurements.

Understanding Conception Date

Conception date refers to when a sperm fertilizes an egg, leading to pregnancy. This typically occurs during ovulation when an egg is released from the ovary.

Key facts about conception:

  • Conception usually occurs about 11-21 days after the first day of your last period
  • The exact date of conception is often difficult to determine
  • Pregnancy dating typically uses the last menstrual period rather than conception date
  • Sperm can live inside the female body for up to 5 days
  • An egg is viable for about 12-24 hours after ovulation

If you know your due date, conception likely occurred approximately 38 weeks before that date.

What is a Full Pregnancy Plan?

A full pregnancy plan helps you understand what to expect throughout your pregnancy journey, from early symptoms to delivery preparation.

What a pregnancy plan includes:

  • Important milestones in fetal development
  • Recommended prenatal appointments and tests
  • Nutritional needs during each trimester
  • Common symptoms and how to manage them
  • Preparation for labor and delivery
  • Postpartum planning

Benefits of having a pregnancy plan:

  • Helps you feel more prepared and in control
  • Ensures you don't miss important medical appointments
  • Provides guidance on healthy pregnancy practices
  • Helps you make informed decisions about your care

Remember that every pregnancy is unique, and your plan should be flexible to accommodate changes.
